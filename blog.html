How to Use Favorites to Save Your Games

July 2026

We've all been there — you discover an amazing game, play it for hours, and then forget its name the next day. That's exactly why we built the Favorites system on EggerMath. It's a simple yet powerful way to save the games you love so you can always find them again.

How to Add a Game to Favorites: Look for the star button (⭐) on any game card throughout the website. When you click it, the star fills in with a golden color, indicating the game has been added to your favorites. You can also remove a game from favorites by clicking the star again — it's a toggle.

Where to Find Your Favorites: Once you've saved some games, click the "Favorites" tab in the category bar at the top of the page. This will filter the game grid to show only your favorited games, making it easy to jump back into your favorites without scrolling through the entire collection.

Synced Across Sessions: Your favorites are saved to your account using an anonymous identifier, which means they persist across browser sessions. Close your browser, come back tomorrow, and your favorites will still be there waiting for you.

On the Game Page: You can also favorite a game from the individual game page. Just click the star icon next to the like/dislike buttons below the game window. This makes it easy to save games while you're playing them.

Sharing Favorites: While favorites are personal to each user, you can always share individual game links with friends. Send them a game URL and they can add it to their own favorites list.

Pro Tip: Use favorites to build collections for different moods. Save puzzle games for when you want to think, racing games for when you want excitement, and clicker games for when you want something relaxing. Your favorites list becomes your personal game library.

The favorites system is completely free and requires no account creation. Just start clicking stars and building your collection. It's that simple!

Best Puzzle Games for Brain Training

July 2026

Puzzle games are more than just entertainment — they're a fantastic way to keep your mind sharp, improve problem-solving skills, and take productive breaks during work or study sessions. EggerMath's puzzle category is packed with challenging games that will test your logic, pattern recognition, and strategic thinking.

2048 — The classic number puzzle that took the internet by storm. Swipe tiles in four directions, and when two identical numbers collide, they merge into one. The goal? Reach the elusive 2048 tile. It sounds simple, but the strategy runs deep. Every move matters, and planning ahead is essential for high scores.

Sudoku — The timeless number-placement puzzle that has captivated millions worldwide. Fill a 9×9 grid so that each column, row, and 3×3 sub-grid contains all digits from 1 to 9. Our digital version offers multiple difficulty levels, from beginner to expert, making it perfect for all skill levels.

Mahjong — A classic tile-matching game with roots in Chinese culture. Match identical tiles to remove them from the board, but be careful — you can only match tiles that are free on at least one side. With hundreds of layouts available, Mahjong offers virtually unlimited replay value.

Block Puzzle — A Tetris-inspired grid puzzle where you place blocks on a board and clear lines by filling complete rows or columns. It combines spatial reasoning with strategic placement, creating an addictive gameplay loop that's hard to put down.

Word Search — Find hidden words in a grid of letters. This classic puzzle game is perfect for vocabulary building and pattern recognition. Words can be hidden horizontally, vertically, or diagonally, adding layers of challenge.

CrossWord — Test your vocabulary and general knowledge with crossword puzzles. Fill in the blanks using clues, and watch as the interconnected grid comes together. It's a great way to learn new words and facts while having fun.

Memory Match — Flip cards to find matching pairs. This classic memory game exercises your short-term memory and concentration. Start with fewer cards for a quick game, or challenge yourself with larger grids for a serious brain workout.

Jigsaw Puzzle — Assemble scattered pieces into a complete image. Our jigsaw puzzles range from simple 50-piece puzzles to complex 500-piece challenges. It's a relaxing yet engaging activity that improves spatial awareness.

Our puzzle games are perfect for quick brain breaks. Whether you have 5 minutes or an hour, there's always a puzzle waiting to challenge you. Each game helps improve different cognitive skills — from logical thinking and pattern recognition to memory and spatial awareness.

All puzzle games on EggerMath are completely free. Just click and start training your brain!

.IO Games: The Multiplayer Craze

July 2026

If you haven't heard of .IO games yet, you're missing out on one of the biggest trends in browser gaming. These multiplayer games have taken the internet by storm, and EggerMath has a fantastic collection ready for you to play.

What Are .IO Games? .IO games are multiplayer browser games that typically feature simple mechanics, competitive gameplay, and real-time competition against other players from around the world. The ".IO" domain extension originally came from the British Indian Ocean Territory, but game developers adopted it because it was short, memorable, and available. Today, it's become synonymous with a genre of fast-paced, accessible multiplayer games.

Why Are They So Popular? Several factors make .IO games incredibly appealing. First, they're completely free and require no downloads. Second, they offer instant multiplayer — you're matched with other real players immediately. Third, the simple controls mean anyone can pick up and play. Finally, the competitive nature creates addictive "one more round" gameplay.

Popular .IO Games on EggerMath:

Our .IO category features some of the best multiplayer experiences available in the browser. These games pit you against dozens or even hundreds of other players in real-time competitions. The gameplay loops are typically simple to learn but difficult to master — eat, grow, conquer, survive.

Whether you prefer strategic territory control, combat-focused gameplay, or cooperative survival, there's a .IO game for you. The real-time multiplayer aspect adds a level of excitement that single-player games simply can't match — every opponent is a real person with their own strategies and skills.

Tips for .IO Games: Start by understanding the basic mechanics before going aggressive. Most .IO games reward patient, strategic play over reckless aggression. Learn the map, understand the power-ups, and develop your own strategies. Watch how top players move and adapt their techniques to improve your own gameplay.

The Social Experience: One of the best things about .IO games is the social aspect. Play with friends by sharing the game link, compete on leaderboards, and become part of the community. Many .IO games have developed passionate communities with their own strategies, tier lists, and competitive scenes.

Ready to join the multiplayer revolution? Head to our .IO category and jump into the action. Whether you're a casual player or a competitive gamer, these games offer endless entertainment.
